Futurense Academy
Monthly Report
April to mid-May 2023
Top Initiatives & Key Highlights
Training
Successfully decreased the overall training duration by 3.5 hours without compromising the quality of the DS program by increasing the training duration by 30 mins on weekdays and by 1 hours on Saturdays, making progress towards our goal of a 78-day training period
The average FFI has been increased to 7.25 from 6.93 in April month across all cohorts by introducing cheat sheets, self-paced courses in training, revamping the difficulty level across assessments, increasing the frequency of mock interviews, providing personalised remediations by Sowmya and Devi that shows significant progress towards our target of 7.4.
Transitioned weekly assessments to 100% scenario based MCQs and compound coding questions from knowledge-based MCQs and direct coding questions, providing a more accurate reflection of candidates' performance.
Utilized blended learning approaches by curating and assigning 5 self-paced learning courses on Data Visualization (Power BI, Tableau) and Azure DE Services (Data Lake, Data Bricks, Data Factory) that allowed engineers to learn at their own pace.
Monitored YTD FFI, increasing the current average to 7.25 across all cohorts, showing significant progress towards our target of 7.4.
Cohort & Engagement
Launched 2 new cohorts
Lateral cohort (FFL-21) has joined us on 16th April at the Hyderabad office, consisting of 13 engineers.
Fresher cohort-University (FFF-22) has joined us on 08th May virtually as their semester exams are going on and will be later transitioned to Hyderabad office after a month. This batch consists of 31 engineers in total.
A new LEM, Deep Chand has joined our Hyderabad office who would be handling FFL-21 and FFF-22 and Vinayak would be mentoring him.
Successfully moved 100% of learners from red zone in 4th week of April for the first time. This was done by connecting with all the engineers individually on a weekly basis to understand their issues and concerns and providing personalised feedback and resources. Follow-up connects conducted with the same engineers to monitor their progress.
Organized 4 alumni connects between existing cohort engineers with deployed engineers from Aditya Birla, Gallagher, Celebal Technologies and Bread Financials where they shared their experiences, including their journey with the company, interview preparation insights, and discussed their current projects at client companies.
Introduced an industry-expert connect program from Rakuten, providing learners with unique insights into the evolving data engineering field. Planning to get the next SME connect sessions with Anees Merchant on Prompt Engineering next month.
Initiated a practice of reaching out to 2 deployed candidates per day. This is helping us to understand the projects these engineers are working on, and also creating a better engagement with the engineers.
Conducted a 34 hours sprint Techathon for 76 students of JAIN final year AIML & Cybersecurity students out of which 3 students were finally selected for a Project Semester with Futurense. Planning to strategize Techathon for Futurense cohorts every month to improve their competitive coding and presentation skills.
