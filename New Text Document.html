Challenges and Learnings
There has been issues faced by the engineers in completing project/assignments within the given timeframe of 1.5 weeks during a recent PySpark training.
To tackle this challenge and improve their productivity, we introduced the Pomodoro Technique by dividing their work into 25-minute intervals with 5-minute breaks in between. The goal is also to teach them effective time management.
There's been pushback from some engineers on the platform regarding new assessment formats due to factors like increased difficulty levels, working on a compiler, and the duration of the tests.
To address these concerns, we're persisting with our mentoring efforts and encouraging practice more hands-on and self-paced resources. The compiler issue is also being addressed by the technology team.
There was a disparity in Python knowledge among the students, with the majority being recent college graduates who had a basic understanding, while a few others who had completed their master's degrees already had an advanced level of proficiency in Python. Also a fair number of students came from support background, with basic level and were slow to pick up advanced levels.
To bridge this gap, we implemented a solution where lower-level students received extra remediation assistance, while advanced students were presented with challenging tasks like RFPs.
